Huricane Preparedness

Are you prepared for a Hurricane?

With hurricane season starting in June and ending in November, the threat of hurricanes in South Florida are very real. Are you and your home ready for a major hurricane? Create a checklist to make sure you have everything you need!

Stock Up on Food and Water

Although there is no reason to panic buy everything at the grocery store, you do want to make sure you are well prepared for the upcoming hurricane. You should have at least a three-day supply of non-perishable food and water. A good rule of thumb with water is to have one gallon of water per person per day. Avoid drinking any tap water at this time, but if absolutely necessary make sure to boil any water before consumption.

Have a First Aid Kit

Every household should have a well-equipped first aid kit in their possession in the case of a hurricane. Some of the items that should be included in this kit should be bandages, gauze pads, tweezers, antiseptic, gloves, and soap.

Put Up Window Shutters

With hurricane force winds and flying debris, you want your windows protected. Some houses in South Florida are equipped with sliding hurricane shutters which are used to easily cover windows while others require a little more work to be manually put on. No matter what type of shutters you have, it is important to get your shutters up as soon as a hurricane watch is put into effect in your area.

Keep Important Documents Safe

Any important documents that you wish to keep safe should be kept in a sealed waterproof container that can be stored in a dry and easily accessible area. Some documents you may want to store are certificates, identification cards, medical records, and insurance information. For those who want to be prepared for any emergency, there are waterproof and fireproof document boxes available in which these documents can be stored in at all times. For another level of safety, any document you wish to protect should also be electronically scanned in and saved.

Know Your Flooding Zones

Knowing the flooding zones around your house is an important part of preparing for a hurricane in South Florida. Most of the state is under a flood warning during any strong storm, therefore knowing how likely your homes elevation and how much rain it would take to flood is important in helping prepare for any storm. There are various online tools to help find your flooding zones, our favorite being eyesontherise.org!

Secure Electronics

In the scenario that your house does flood, you want all of the electronics in your house to be secure, this meaning that all electronics are unplugged and stored up high to avoid any contact with water. If any electronic devices are small enough, store them in an airtight waterproof bag.
